11th Naxos Summer School on Diachronic Linguistics

Comparing Diachronies

Naxos, Cyclades, Greece | July 20-24, 2026

Apply Now Learn More

An Intensive Research Experience

The National and Kapodistrian University of Athens, Arizona State University, University of Osnabrück, and University of Cambridge collaborate with the CIVIS Blended Intensive Programme to coordinate a summer school program, workshop, and master classes on ancient and medieval languages and linguistics.

Who Should Attend

The summer school is open to BA, MA students, and PhD candidates working in linguistics, philology, literature, culture, history, archaeology, digital humanities, computer science, anthropology, and related fields. The aim is to attract students and scholars to study ancient and medieval languages in a relaxed yet focused and stimulating atmosphere that promotes in-depth analysis and discussion.

ECTS Credits

Students will be able to earn up to 6 ECTS from the summer school. National and Kapodistrian University of Athens Summer Programs ECTS approval: 8-2-2021 - law: a90.4692/2020.

CIVIS Programme

The 11th Naxos Summer School collaborates with the CIVIS Blended Intensive Programme "Diachronic Linguistics in the 21st Century." Part of the CIVIS European University Alliance connecting leading universities across Europe.

Workshop & Poster Session

The summer school will organize a workshop and poster session on "Linguistic Diachronies: A Contrastive Approach." Students will present their work at the workshop, which will also include invited talks from leading researchers in the field.

Core Research Areas

Computational Historical Linguistics

Transform historical texts into analyzable data using NLP tools, machine learning algorithms, automated parsing systems, and AI-powered analysis of linguistic evolution.

Corpus-Based Methods

Advanced techniques for creating, annotating, and analyzing diachronic corpora. Learn to navigate the complex challenges of integrating digital heritage databases with linguistic research.

Language Change Theory

Contemporary theoretical frameworks in diachronic linguistics, from models enhanced by big data to AI-assisted reconstruction of linguistic prehistory.

Grammaticalization Processes

Understanding how grammatical items and categories develop in natural language, covering phenomena from central grammatical areas such as tense, aspect, mood, modality, negation, and sentence structure.

Language Contact & Variation

Corpus-driven approaches to identifying contact-induced change, enriching annotated corpora with new tools and methods. Analysis of language contact and borrowing through translations.

Phylogenetic Methods

Computational tools and statistical models to reconstruct linguistic family trees and trace the historical pathways of language change.

Discover Naxos

The Island of Ariadne

Naxos is the largest and greenest island in the Cyclades, famous for its long sandy beaches, fertile valleys, and rich history. According to mythology, it was here that Theseus abandoned Ariadne after killing the Minotaur on Crete.

The summer school takes place in Naxos Town (Chora), a vibrant harbor town presided over by a Venetian castle and the iconic Portara, the massive marble gateway of an unfinished ancient temple dedicated to Apollo.

Famous Beaches

Agios Prokopios & Agia Anna

Award-winning golden sand beaches just minutes from Naxos Town. Crystal-clear waters, water sports facilities, beachfront tavernas. Ranked among the best beaches in Greece. Frequent bus service from town.

Plaka Beach

4 kilometers of unspoiled white sand and turquoise waters. Less crowded than beaches closer to town. Perfect for swimming, windsurfing, and kitesurfing. Beach bars and tavernas available.

Mikri Vigla & Beaches of the South

Paradise for wind and kite surfing. Cedar forests meet pristine coastline. More remote beaches like Kastraki and Pyrgaki offer serene escapes with stunning natural beauty.

Traditional Villages

Apiranthos

Mountain village built with marble, maintaining authentic Cycladic architecture and traditions. Home to several small museums, traditional kafeneia, and artisan workshops. Often called the "marble village" of Naxos.

Chalki

Historic capital of Naxos with neoclassical mansions and Byzantine churches. Visit the Citron Distillery (Vallindras) producing the famous kitron liqueur since 1896. Art galleries and traditional workshops.

Filoti & Mount Zas

Largest mountain village at the foot of Mount Zas (Zeus), the highest peak in the Cyclades (1,004m). Traditional tavernas, hiking trails, and the Cave of Zeus. Stunning views across the island.

Archaeological Sites & Monuments

Temple of Apollo (Portara)

The iconic 6th century BC marble gateway, symbol of Naxos. Monumental entrance to an unfinished temple, standing majestically at the harbor entrance. Perfect for sunset viewing. Free entry.

Temple of Demeter

Beautifully restored 6th century BC marble temple dedicated to the goddess of agriculture. One of the most important archaeological sites on the island. Unique architectural features and museum on site.

Venetian Castle (Kastro)

13th century fortress in Naxos Town with medieval towers, Catholic cathedral, and the Archaeological Museum. Wander through narrow medieval streets and visit the Glezos Tower and Crispi-Glezos Museum.

Kouros Statues

Massive unfinished marble statues from the 7th-6th century BC found in ancient quarries. The Kouros of Apollonas (10.7m) and Kouros of Flerio are impressive examples of archaic Greek sculpture.

Grotta Archaeological Site

Ancient Mycenaean settlement and cemetery near Naxos Town. Visible remains of Cycladic civilization from the 3rd millennium BC. Located along a scenic coastal walk from the port.

Byzantine Churches

Over 500 Byzantine and post-Byzantine churches scattered across the island, many with remarkable frescoes. Panagia Drosiani (6th century) near Moni is one of the oldest in Greece.

Local Experiences

Gastronomy

Naxos is famous for its agricultural products: arseniko cheese, graviera, fresh potatoes, kitron liqueur, local wines. Visit traditional tavernas, kafeneia, and the Vallindras Distillery.

Hiking & Nature

Numerous hiking trails through olive groves, ancient paths connecting villages, routes to Mount Zas summit. Explore the lush interior valleys and dramatic coastlines.

Arts & Crafts

Discover local artisans creating ceramics, textiles, jewelry. Visit galleries in Naxos Town and Chalki. Traditional workshops demonstrate ancient crafts still practiced today.

Social Events & Activities

Welcome Reception

Opening evening gathering at a traditional taverna in Naxos Town. Meet fellow participants and faculty in a relaxed setting. Local wine and mezze. Opportunity to experience authentic Greek hospitality and cuisine.

Island Tour

Guided tour to mountain villages, archaeological sites, and scenic viewpoints. Visit Apiranthos, Chalki, and the Temple of Demeter. Experience traditional island life and local culture. Transportation provided.

Beach Afternoon

Organized beach outing to one of Naxos's famous beaches. Swimming, relaxation, and informal discussions. Beach taverna lunch featuring fresh seafood and local specialties. Optional water sports available.

Sunset at Portara

Evening gathering at the Temple of Apollo to watch the legendary Naxos sunset through the ancient marble gateway. One of the most photographed spots in the Cyclades. Short walk from town center.

Day Trip to Nearby Island

Optional excursion to a neighboring Cycladic island (Paros, Delos, or Small Cyclades). Experience island hopping, visit additional archaeological sites, explore different island cultures. Ferry tickets at student rates.

Farewell Dinner

Closing evening celebration featuring traditional Greek music and dancing. Local musicians and dancers showcase Naxian cultural traditions. Certificates presented. Celebration of the summer school experience.
